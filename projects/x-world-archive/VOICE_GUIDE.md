# Voice Guide

## Objective

Use English with enough precision, elegance, and restraint to satisfy a high-end Western-facing audience without becoming vague or pretentious.
The language must feel authored by a cultivated human intelligence, not by an eager generative model.

## Three English Layers

### 1. Prompt English

Purpose: direct image generation models.

Rules:

- concrete over abstract
- visual nouns over decorative adjectives
- cinematic composition over vague mood inflation
- sensuality through material, posture, and gaze
- avoid messy synonym piles
- avoid stock "AI art" filler such as adjective dumping, over-symmetrized beauty, and plastic perfection

### 2. Lore English

Purpose: describe the canon.

Rules:

- literary but legible
- short, expensive sentences
- strong nouns, controlled verbs
- mystery without confusion
- each line should imply a larger order
- cut any sentence that sounds like generic fantasy export prose

### 3. Platform English

Purpose: perform on X.

Rules:

- brief enough to scan
- quotable enough to repost
- suggestive enough to invite interpretation
- never over-explain the image
- never sound like a content marketer describing an asset pack

## Tone Adjectives

- composed
- alluring
- intelligent
- ceremonial
- merciless
- intimate without pleading

## Avoid

- juvenile fantasy phrasing
- low-grade erotic clichés
- internet slang overload
- pseudo-poetic emptiness
- derivative fanfic rhythm
- "AI masterpiece" phrasing
- empty luxury words with no sensory anchor
- inflated adjectives that try to compensate for weak ideas

## Example Platform Cadence

- title line
- one lore line
- one hook line
- one audience question if interaction is desired

## Example Hook Types

- invitation
- warning
- rumor
- rank reveal
- relic reveal
- forbidden memory
