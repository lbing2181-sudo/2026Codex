# World Bible

## Master Premise

`The Sable Archive` is a fictional imperial archive made of sealed dossiers, forbidden portraits, relic notes, court whispers, and ceremonial records from multiple sister realms.

The archive is not presented all at once.
It is unveiled as if the audience is being allowed to read what should have remained closed.

## Structural Model

The universe runs on three layers.

### Layer 1: Supreme Universe

One master archive identity holds the entire canon together.

### Layer 2: Public Seasonal Realm

Only one primary realm should dominate the public account at a time.
For season one, that realm is `The Court of Perfume and Dust`.

### Layer 3: Sealed Expansion Realms

Other worlds exist in canon but are introduced slowly through fragments, rumors, artifacts, or diplomatic references rather than full launch behavior.

## Season One Realm

### Name

`The Court of Perfume and Dust`

### Realm Qualities

- desert empire atmosphere
- perfumed palaces
- caravan wealth
- court intrigue
- veiled ceremony
- heat, gold, shadow, incense

### Narrative Promise

The audience enters through images of women, but stays for the court, the codes, the rivalries, the ornaments, the cities, and the sense that every face belongs to a deeper order.

## Sealed Realms

### The Salt Throne

A maritime imperial frontier shaped by salt air, silver rites, harbor queens, and storm diplomacy.

### The Velvet House

A decadent urban court of velvet chambers, merchant aristocracy, and polished cruelty.

### The Nocturne Cloister

A cold ecclesiastical realm of vows, candles, stone silence, and forbidden tenderness.

### The Brass Crescent

A machine-age ceremonial state of brass, smoke, gears, and moonlit engineering nobility.

## Archive Classes

Every published asset should belong to one of these classes:

1. `Dossier`: a character entry
2. `Fragment`: a short lore shard
3. `Relic`: an object-centered post
4. `Edict`: a political or ceremonial rule
5. `Whisper`: a seductive one-line transmission
6. `Scene`: a cinematic moment
7. `Article`: a longform archive file

## Character Doctrine

- Characters must feel adult, sovereign, and psychologically legible.
- Beauty alone is not enough; every woman must imply rank, history, or danger.
- Body language should signal self-possession, invitation, judgment, distance, or ritual.

## Expansion Doctrine

- The audience should suspect a larger world before they are shown it.
- New realms should enter as controlled revelation, not as abrupt category switches.
- Expansion is earned when the first realm is memorable.
