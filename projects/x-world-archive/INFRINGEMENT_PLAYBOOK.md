# Infringement Playbook

## Objective

Respond quickly and coherently when `Sable Archive` assets are copied, reposted, impersonated, or diluted by lookalike accounts.

## Core Principle

Speed matters.
Documentation matters.
Emotion does not help.

## Threat Types

### 1. Direct Image Theft

Someone reposts the exact image or a near-identical crop.

### 2. Caption or Article Theft

Someone copies original text, lore fragments, or dossier structure.

### 3. Impersonation

Someone imitates the account name, emblem, or archive positioning closely enough to confuse audiences.

### 4. Derivative Copycat Behavior

Someone imitates the package aggressively without exact duplication.

This is harder to enforce legally, but still worth logging.

## First Response Checklist

1. capture screenshots
2. capture the account handle and URL
3. capture the post URL
4. archive timestamps
5. identify which asset ID is affected
6. collect the evidence package
7. decide whether the issue is copyright, impersonation, or both

## Enforcement Packet

Prepare a packet containing:

- original asset file
- hashes
- publication URL
- publication date
- screenshot of the infringement
- screenshot of the original
- corresponding dossier or post record
- concise statement of ownership and conflict

## Escalation Path

### Level 1

Internal logging only.

Use when:

- the imitation is weak
- the account is low impact
- the evidence is not worth immediate action

### Level 2

Platform report.

Use when:

- the asset is clearly copied
- the account is gaining traction
- there is direct confusion or theft

### Level 3

Repeat infringement tracking.

Use when:

- the same account repeats behavior
- multiple accounts are copying the same canon package
- brand confusion becomes likely

## Platform Categories

Typical report pathways:

- copyright infringement
- intellectual property violation
- impersonation or misleading identity

## Internal Rule

Do not improvise from memory.
Use the evidence chain and asset IDs.

## Long-Term Defense

The strongest defense is not only reporting.
It is becoming the obvious original source through:

- stronger documentation
- faster release logs
- more coherent brand identity
- deeper canon that imitators cannot fully replicate
