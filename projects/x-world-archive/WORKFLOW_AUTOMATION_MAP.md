# Workflow Automation Map

## Objective

Map the full `Sable Archive` workflow and identify what should be automated, what should be batched, and what must remain under direct human judgment.

## Core Thesis

The project should feel highly authored in public and highly systematized in private.

That means:

- automate repetition
- batch production
- preserve human control over taste, canon, and final selection

## Workflow Layers

### 1. Canon Planning

Tasks:

- choose realm focus
- choose archive classes
- choose recurring women
- decide content mix

Automation potential:

- medium

Can be assisted by templates and planning sheets, but final direction should remain human-led.

### 2. Prompt Assembly

Tasks:

- combine structured prompt layers
- pull character dossier anchors
- assign archive metadata

Automation potential:

- high

This is a strong candidate for reusable prompt builders and templates.

### 3. Image Generation

Tasks:

- run batch generations
- produce multiple variants
- organize candidates

Automation potential:

- high

This is one of the best batch-processing layers.

### 4. Selection

Tasks:

- pick what feels authored
- reject generic outputs
- preserve continuity

Automation potential:

- low

This is the taste bottleneck and should remain human-supervised.

### 5. Caption and Lore Drafting

Tasks:

- generate X caption drafts
- generate dossier lines
- generate article fragments

Automation potential:

- high with review

Drafting can be accelerated heavily, but final language must be curated for tone and de-AI flavor.

### 6. Asset Logging

Tasks:

- assign IDs
- save prompts
- save hashes
- store file paths
- register publish status

Automation potential:

- very high

This should eventually become mostly automatic.

### 7. Publishing Preparation

Tasks:

- crop versions
- platform-specific caption variants
- post scheduling packets

Automation potential:

- high

### 8. Motion Adaptation

Tasks:

- select stills for motion
- create short movement prompts
- export short teasers

Automation potential:

- medium to high

But selection should remain manual.

### 9. Enforcement and Archiving

Tasks:

- monitor copying
- collect proof
- prepare report packets

Automation potential:

- medium

Detection can be assisted.
Interpretation and escalation should remain deliberate.

## Best Batch Strategy

The best operating model is not daily improvisation.
It is concentrated production blocks.

## Recommended Cadence

### One-Day Weekly Batch

Possible output:

- 5 to 10 still-image posts
- 10 to 20 caption candidates
- 2 to 4 fragment posts
- 1 to 2 motion candidates

### One-Day Biweekly Batch

Possible output:

- 10 to 16 still-image posts
- 20 to 30 caption candidates
- 4 to 6 fragment posts
- 3 to 5 motion candidates
- 1 article outline

### Intensive Monthly Batch

Possible output:

- most of a month of still posts
- a motion bank
- a dossier bank
- a scheduled archive queue

## What Should Be Automated First

1. prompt assembly from templates
2. asset naming and ID assignment
3. file hashing and evidence logging
4. caption variant generation
5. platform-specific packaging

## What Should Never Be Fully Automated

1. final selection
2. canon approval
3. recurring character continuity judgment
4. public-facing final language
5. strategy changes

## Long-Term System Goal

The user should eventually be able to spend one concentrated day to prepare one to four weeks of controlled content, while the repository preserves memory, evidence, and continuity for future operators.
